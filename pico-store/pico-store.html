The PICO Story

A house built on the weight of cloth.

PICO began with a sari folded inside a wooden almirah — a wedding gift, never worn, kept for thirty years. We saw a question in it: how do we let the fabric speak again, in a form our daughters might wear on a Tuesday?

Why we exist

We believe the most beautiful textiles in the world have already been woven. They sit in trunks, between layers of muslin, in the wardrobes of mothers and grandmothers. PICO is the bridge between those textiles and the wardrobe of a modern person who wants to wear them — not preserve them.

We are slow, intentionally. We make small numbers and we put names on labels. We pay our weavers fairly. We don't promise sustainability as a marketing line — we promise it as a working method.

House Principles

Six quiet commitments

I

Honour the textile

The fabric leads. The silhouette serves it.

II

Pay above market

For the weavers, the cutters, the finishers.

III

Slow over fast

Three to six weeks per commission. Always.

IV

Name the maker

Every piece is signed by the person who finished it.

V

Return the offcuts

What's yours stays yours. We send the remnants back.

VI

Repair, forever

Bring it back any year, any decade. We mend.

VII

Document everything

Each piece comes with the maker's note and the textile's origin.

VIII

Quiet over loud

No logos on the outside. Heritage, never branded.

The Craft

From cloth to keepsake.

A PICO commission moves through four hands and seven inspections. Below is what happens to your fabric, in order, after it leaves your home.

01

Receiving

Every fabric is photographed, weighed, and assigned an atelier number on arrival.

02

Suitability review

Master tailor inspects weave strength, fibre direction, and damage. We send an honest note.

03

Pattern study

Pattern is mapped to honour the motifs — borders preserved, palla aligned.

04

Cutting

Hand-cut by senior cutters. Never on a table you've not seen.

05

Construction

Bound seams, mulberry silk lining, hand-finished interiors.

06

Hardware

Solid brass, bone, bridle leather. Made to age.

07

Quality check

Seven-point inspection. Two senior makers must sign off.

08

Wrap & send

Mulberry tissue, hand-stamped box, the maker's note, the offcuts returned.

After-Care

How to live with a PICO piece.

Wear it.

Heirlooms grow more beautiful with use. Don't archive ours.

Brush, don't wash.

Soft horsehair brush, in the direction of the weave.

Hang in muslin.

Breathable fabric beats plastic, every season.

Send it back.

For mending, for refresh, for re-lining. Forever.
